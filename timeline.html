610 – 613 CE

Chronicles of the Secret Da'wah

Tracing the three foundational years of Islamic propagation within the close circles of Makkah — before a single word was spoken in public.

610 CE — Ramadan

The First Revelation, Cave Hira

At forty years of age, while in spiritual retreat in the cave of Hira on Jabal an-Nur, the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was visited by the Angel Jibril (Gabriel). He was commanded: "Read! In the name of your Lord who created." (Surah Al-Alaq, 96:1–5). Overwhelmed, he returned home trembling to his wife Khadija (RA), who comforted him, wrapped him in a cloak, and took him to her cousin Waraqah ibn Nawfal — a learned Christian scholar — who confirmed that this was the same revelation sent to Musa (Moses).

610 CE

The Pause and the Command to Warn

A period of silence followed the first revelation, known as the Fatrah, during which the Prophet ﷺ longed for further guidance. Revelation resumed with Surah Al-Muddaththir: "O you wrapped in your cloak! Arise and warn." (74:1–2). This marked the formal beginning of his mission — though, by divine wisdom, the call would remain private for the next three years.

610 CE

Khadija (RA) — The First to Believe

Before anyone else on earth, Khadija bint Khuwaylid (RA) affirmed her husband's prophethood without hesitation. Her wealth, status, and unwavering belief gave the new message its first foundation of safety and emotional strength.

610 CE

The Household Believes — Ali and Zaid

Within the Prophet's ﷺ own household, two more hearts accepted the truth almost immediately: Ali ibn Abi Talib (RA), his young cousin being raised in his care, and Zaid ibn Harithah (RA), his freed slave and adopted son. Their early acceptance showed that the message resonated across every generation and social position from its very first day.

610 CE

Abu Bakr (RA) and the Quiet Network of Da'wah

Abu Bakr as-Siddiq (RA), a respected and trusted Makkan merchant, accepted Islam without a moment's doubt — becoming the first adult male believer from outside the Prophet's household. His impeccable reputation and wide circle of friendships became the single most effective channel through which Islam spread quietly among Makkah's most capable young men.

610 – 611 CE

The Circle Widens — Da'wah Sirriyyah (The Secret Call)

Through Abu Bakr's careful, one-on-one invitations, a group of young Makkans — later honoured among As-Sabiqun al-Awwalun, the Foremost Forerunners — entered the faith: Uthman ibn Affan, Zubayr ibn al-Awwam, Abdur Rahman ibn Awf, Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas, and Talha ibn Ubaydullah. No sermons were preached, no gatherings held in the open. Invitations moved through trust: kinsman to kinsman, friend to friend.

611 CE

Persecution Begins — The Weak Are Tested First

As word of the new faith reached beyond trusted circles, Makkah's powerful elite turned their hostility first toward believers who had no tribal protection — slaves and the poor. Bilal ibn Rabah, the family of Yasir ibn Amir, and Khabbab ibn al-Aratt endured public torture for refusing to renounce their faith, even as the Prophet's ﷺ own household remained largely shielded by clan custom.

612 CE

Dar al-Arqam — A House Becomes a School

As the number of believers grew, the Prophet ﷺ chose the home of a young companion, Al-Arqam ibn Abi al-Arqam, at the foot of Mount Safa, as a secret meeting place. Here the Qur'an was taught, prayer was learned, and a fragile young community was nurtured safely, away from the eyes of Quraysh.

613 CE

The Call at Safa — The Secret Era Ends

After three years of quiet, careful growth, revelation came commanding open proclamation: "So proclaim what you are commanded and turn away from the polytheists." (Surah Al-Hijr, 15:94). The Prophet ﷺ climbed Mount Safa and called out to all of Makkah by clan name — and the public phase of Islam's history began.
