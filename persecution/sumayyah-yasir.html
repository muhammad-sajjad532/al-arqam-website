The First Martyrs

Sumayyah & Yasir (RA)

A husband and wife who paid the first price of faith, and never once renounced it.

← Back to Trials & Sacrifice

A Family Without Protection

Yasir ibn Amir had migrated to Makkah from Yemen and, lacking a clan of his own, entered into an alliance with the Banu Makhzum tribe. He married Sumayyah bint Khabbat, a formerly enslaved woman, and together they had a son, Ammar. As outsiders without tribal standing, the family had no one to shield them when Makkah's elite turned violent against the early believers.

Singled Out by Abu Jahl

When the family's acceptance of Islam became known, Abu Jahl — one of the fiercest opponents of the Prophet ﷺ — made them a target of relentless cruelty. They were dragged out into the scorching midday heat of the desert, on the burning sands outside Makkah, and tortured in an attempt to force them to renounce their faith. Each time the Prophet ﷺ passed by and saw their suffering, unable to stop it, he could only offer them words that have echoed through Islamic history ever since.

"Patience, O family of Yasir, for your appointed place is Paradise." — The Prophet ﷺ

Sumayyah (RA) — The First Martyr of Islam

Sumayyah bint Khabbat (RA) refused, to her last breath, to speak a single word against her faith. In his fury at her unbroken resolve, Abu Jahl killed her with a spear — making her the very first martyr in the history of Islam. Her steadfastness in the face of death became a permanent symbol of conviction for every generation of Muslims that followed.

Yasir (RA) — The Second Martyr

Yasir ibn Amir (RA) did not long survive his wife. He died from the torture inflicted upon him, becoming Islam's second martyr. The family's only surviving member, their son Ammar, would carry the memory of his parents' sacrifice for the rest of his long life.

  • Sumayyah (RA) is remembered as the first martyr in Islamic history.
  • Yasir (RA) became the second martyr, dying from his injuries shortly after.
  • Neither renounced their faith, even under torture and the threat of death.
