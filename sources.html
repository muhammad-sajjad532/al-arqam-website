Historical Sources

This archive is built on the classical works of Islamic scholarship that have preserved the Seerah for over fourteen centuries.

Hadith Collection

Sahih al-Bukhari

Compiled by Imam Muhammad al-Bukhari, this is regarded as the most authentic collection of hadith in Sunni Islam, and the primary source for many of the narrations referenced throughout this archive, including the account of the first revelation.

Hadith Collection

Sahih Muslim

Compiled by Imam Muslim ibn al-Hajjaj, this collection ranks alongside Sahih al-Bukhari as one of the two most trusted hadith compilations, providing further corroboration for the events of the early Da'wah.

Earliest Biography

As-Seerah an-Nabawiyyah

Ibn Hisham's recension of Ibn Ishaq's original biography of the Prophet ﷺ, written in the 8th century, is the earliest surviving connected narrative of his life and remains foundational to all later Seerah scholarship.

Classical History

Tarikh al-Tabari

Imam at-Tabari's monumental history places the events of the secret Da'wah within the broader chronicle of early Islamic and world history, cross-referencing dates and details against other early sources.

Companion Biographies

Al-Isabah fi Tamyiz as-Sahabah

Ibn Hajar al-Asqalani's comprehensive biographical dictionary of the companions, used to verify the individual life details of figures such as Khadija, Bilal, Sumayyah, and Khabbab (RA).

Modern Scholarship

Ar-Raheeq Al-Makhtum

Safi-ur-Rahman al-Mubarakpuri's award-winning modern biography of the Prophet ﷺ, widely used today to present the classical Seerah sources in an accessible, structured narrative.

A Note on This Archive

This website is an educational presentation of well-established Seerah history, written for students, researchers, and anyone seeking to understand the foundational years of Islam. For matters of religious ruling or detailed scholarly verification, readers are encouraged to consult qualified scholars and the primary texts directly.
