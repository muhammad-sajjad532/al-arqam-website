The First Believer & Pillar

Khadija bint Khuwaylid (RA)

"There is none better for you than her." — The Prophet ﷺ

← Back to all Pioneers

A Woman of Standing

Khadija bint Khuwaylid was among the most respected and successful merchants of Makkah, known across the city for her honesty and business acumen. She had employed the young Muhammad ﷺ — already famed for his trustworthiness, Al-Amin — to lead her trade caravan to Syria. Impressed by his integrity and the remarkable profit he returned with, she proposed marriage. He was twenty-five; she was forty. Their marriage lasted twenty-five years, and for as long as she lived, the Prophet ﷺ took no other wife.

The First to Believe

When the Prophet ﷺ returned from Cave Hira, shaken by his first encounter with the Angel Jibril, it was Khadija (RA) who steadied him. She did not hesitate, did not question, and did not doubt. Before any other soul on earth, she declared her belief in his prophethood. Her response, recorded in the earliest biographies, remains one of the most quoted lines in Islamic history:

"By Allah, never will Allah disgrace you. You keep good relations with kin, help the poor and the destitute, serve your guests generously, and assist those afflicted by calamity." — Khadija (RA), comforting the Prophet ﷺ

The Pillar of the Secret Years

Throughout the three years of secret Da'wah, Khadija's (RA) wealth funded the young community's needs, her social standing offered a measure of protection, and her unwavering certainty gave the Prophet ﷺ the emotional foundation to continue. When persecution intensified in later years and the Muslims were boycotted in the valley of Abu Talib, she shared in every hardship despite her age and status, never once wavering.

  • The first believer in Islam, accepting before any other person.
  • Financial backbone of the early Muslim community for over a decade.
  • Emotional anchor for the Prophet ﷺ through the most difficult years of his mission.
  • Honoured by Muslims as the "Mother of Believers" and a model of loyalty and conviction.
