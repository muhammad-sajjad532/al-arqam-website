Walking the Sacred Ground

Four Locations, One Era

Every location below was part of a single, unbroken story. Within a few miles of each other in Makkah, the first revelation was received, the first believers gathered in secret, and finally — three years later — the call went public. Click any pin on the map to explore what happened there.

About the Map

This map uses real GPS coordinates for each historical location. OpenStreetMap provides the base layer. The modern city of Makkah has grown significantly around these ancient sites — many are now incorporated within or near the Masjid al-Haram complex.

Note on Mount Safa: The public call at Safa in 613 CE marks the end of the secret era this archive documents — it is shown here as a contextual reference point, not as part of the secret Da'wah itself.
Location Summaries

The Four Sites

City Centre Landmark

The Ka'bah

The sacred cube at the heart of Masjid al-Haram and the geographical and spiritual centre of Makkah. The Prophet ﷺ was born into a society organised around it. During the secret years, it was the very power base of Quraysh — whose authority he was quietly, carefully working around.

610 CE · First Revelation

Cave Hira, Jabal an-Nur

About 3 km north-east of the Ka'bah, on Jabal an-Nur (Mountain of Light), sits the narrow cave where the Prophet ﷺ was in spiritual retreat when the Angel Jibril first appeared with the command: "Read!" This is where the secret era began.

612 CE · First School

Dar al-Arqam, Foot of Mount Safa

The house of Al-Arqam ibn Abi al-Arqam, at the foot of Mount Safa. For roughly a year, the Prophet ﷺ taught Qur'an here in secret — the first madrasah in Islamic history. Its strategic location near the Ka'bah yet in an inconspicuous clan's house was deliberate.

613 CE · The Next Chapter

Mount Safa

Just steps from Dar al-Arqam, the rocky hill of Safa is where — after three years of quiet preparation — the Prophet ﷺ climbed and called out to all of Makkah by clan name, beginning the public phase of Islam. This location marks the end of the era this archive covers.
