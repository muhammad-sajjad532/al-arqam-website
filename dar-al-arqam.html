At the Foot of Mount Safa

Dar al-Arqam — The House of Knowledge

An ordinary home that became the headquarters of the first madrasah in Islamic history.

Why This House

A Sanctuary Chosen With Care

As the number of believers grew beyond what could safely meet in any one family's home, the Prophet ﷺ needed a base that would not draw suspicion. He chose the house of a young companion, Al-Arqam ibn Abi al-Arqam, located at the foot of Mount Safa, close to the Ka'bah yet inconspicuous. Critically, Al-Arqam belonged to the Banu Makhzum clan — a tribe with no connection to the Prophet's own clan of Banu Hashim, meaning Quraysh's leaders would have no obvious reason to watch the house for signs of the new faith.

Inside the House

What Happened Within Its Walls

  • Total secrecy: A strategically chosen location away from tribal tension, where believers could gather without drawing the attention of Quraysh.
  • Qur'anic instruction: The Prophet ﷺ personally taught the verses revealed so far, explaining their meaning and recitation.
  • Spiritual formation: Believers learned how to pray, the foundations of moral conduct, and how to live as Muslims in a hostile environment.
  • Equality before faith: Wealthy merchants like Abu Bakr and Uthman, a young boy like Ali, and freed slaves like Zaid and Bilal sat together as equals — a master and a slave studying the same verse, side by side.
  • Brotherhood: A spiritual family was forged in secret conviction, bonds that would carry the community through the persecution and migrations still to come.

Total Secrecy

Strategically chosen, away from tribal tensions and the gaze of Quraysh's leadership.

Spiritual Formation

Establishing the foundations of prayer, Qur'anic recitation, and moral conduct.

Equality

Master and slave sat as brothers before the divine word — Islam's earliest social revolution.

From This House, a Call to All of Makkah

After roughly a year of teaching within these walls, revelation came commanding the Prophet ﷺ to climb Mount Safa — just steps from this very house — and proclaim the message openly for the first time.
